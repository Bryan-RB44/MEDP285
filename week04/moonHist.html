Ancient Viewings

Images: Stone Tablet and Starry Night Painting, Both Depicting the Moon

From the dawn of mankind to right before the advent of the telescope, mankind has always looked up to the night sky at our planet's long time companion: our Moon. We've depicted it in various forms of art throughout our history as it's one of the largest and brightest objects in our skies only second to the Sun. Its brightness and size makes it influential in both our culture and minds as we continue to gaze and stare at it in awe and wonder.

Era of the Telescope

Images: First Drawn Depictions of Telescope Observations of the Moon by Galileo Galilei

Not long after the telescope was invented, people began using this new tool to look up to the sky and see things no person has managed to see before. Galileo Galilei alongside others looked up at the Moon to see its cratered surface, beating the long standing myth that it was a perfect and smooth sphere but instead another world just like ours. This led to mankind mapping out the Moon as much as we could and only imagining what lies on that other world across the void, as it wouldn't be until much later where we would have the chance to do so.

Space Age Exploration

Images (Left to Right): First Image of Far-Side of the Moon, Flight Over the Moon During Apollo Missions, and Intuitive Machines Lunar Lander Camera View

At long last, humanity has reached the Moon! Within the last 100 years we've managed to not only send machines around the Moon but also land on it on several occasions, some soley having machines involved and others having humans touch its surface at long last. These missions helped shape our knowledge on both the Moon's past and what lies in its future. With large plains of cooled magma, we now know our Moon used to be a hot volcanic world born from the collision between Earth and another planet. With collected samples and science from the Apollo missions, we know the Moon is still an active world and how the Lunar environment impacts the human body. With further lander and orbiter missions, we know that there's water on the Moon and caves, making the future of mankind's space exploration a big possibility that all relies on our celestial friend.
