Other Objects

Besides the large bodies out in deep space and in our own neighborhood, we find other things we can see in our night sky. From the most well known to the obscure, here's some other objects that I took a look at and captured for people to see!

Our Moon

Our Moon is our planet's long time friend and everyone has seen it in their lives at least a few times. Nothing beats watching it move in the sky, whether day or night and with or without a telescope. Its changing shape yet simplicity makes it ironically one of the few exciting constants in all our lives, making it something we often take for granted when it's often seen as a dull rock in the sky.

Image Credit: Bryan Briones
Equipment: Skywatcher 130P Telescope, Samsung S8 Camera

Comet 12P/Pons-Brooks

This comet passed by us earlier this year and it didn't disappoint. Comets are large chunks of dust and ice that come from the outer edges of our soler system. This one in particular comes around every 71 years for that very reason, making it feel lucky to have the chance to both see it and share photos of it to those who couldn't see it.

Image Credit: Bryan Briones
Equipment & Software: Zwo Seestar S50, Adobe Lightroom

Dwarf Planets & Asteroids

Often the most underrated bodies in our own solar system, here are the dwarf planets Ceres and Pluto, alongside the massive asteroids Vesta and Pallas. Yes, they're just dots in these pictures as well as viewing for the fact that they are tiny, with all four being the size of Australia or smaller. Regardless, they are still interesting parts to see, capture, and appreciate, at least for me. These are often the farthest and dimmest objects you could see of our solar system from NYC, so it's best to appreciate them too if given the chance to view them.

Image Credit: Bryan Briones
Equipment: Zwo Seestar S50
