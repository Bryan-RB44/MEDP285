Planets

The planets of our neighborhood come in all shapes, sizes, and types, almost all of which can be seen from our light polluted skies! Here are some of the brightest ones that can be seen extremely easily on the months they're out for.

Venus

Named after the goddess of beauty, Venus is a hellish planet and is the 3rd brightest thing in our sky (with only the Moon and the Sun beating it). Seething at temperatures beyond our comprehension, we can safely see our closest neighbor from the comfort of our own lawns/roofs and see its various phases. Never being too far from the Sun, we can see it rise and dive in either the early mornings or late afternoon skies towards the Eastern and Western horizons respectively (Western at the moment of this being typed).

Image Credit: Bryan Briones
Equipment: Skywatcher 130P Telescope, Samsung S8 Camera

Jupiter

This gaseous planet is incredibly far but still very much visible at being the 4th brightest object in the sky, with Jupiter bearing 4 bright moons as well when seen with even binoculars. Its iconic stripes make it great for seeing and imaging with proper gear! (Unfortunately I had awful viewing conditions whenever I did try imaging this planet.)

Image Credit: Bryan Briones
Equipment: Skywatcher 130P Telescope, Celestron Nexstar 8SE, Samsung S8 Camera, Samsung S23+ Camera

Saturn

While not as bright, the ringed Saturn is still easy to spot in our skies and nobody can ever forget the first time ever witnessing the rings in person through a telescope. It alongside its Earth-like moon Titan (seen in the brightened image) are great sights for anyone with a telescope and I managed to get this solid picture with great viewing conditions one lucky night.

Image Credit: Bryan Briones
Equipment: Skywatcher 130P Telescope, Samsung S8 Camera
